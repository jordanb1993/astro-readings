### May 12, 2026 — Intuitive Pull (Dating / Eleanor)
**Deck:** Buffy the Vampire Slayer
**Context:** Night before second date with Eleanor (May 13). Anxiety about whether Eleanor is compatible, whether casual enjoyment needs to be "justified" by seriousness. Intuitive pull — shuffled and allowed what fell out to fall out.
**Orienting questions held while shuffling:** What's going on internally for me during this / How should I approach this situation / What is likely to happen or come from this
**Astrological context:** Neptune 0.38° from exact conjunction natal Moon (emotional field saturated and porous) · Uranus 0.08° from exact opposition natal Sun (identity renovation arriving) · Jupiter 0.09° past opposition natal 7th house Uranus/Neptune (relational window still live) · Balsamic Moon phase (~3.6 days from New Moon) — interior, release-oriented, not launch energy

| # | Card | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Knight of Stakes (Knight of Wands) | Internal state: genuine fire, real attraction, already-moving impulse. The anxiety isn't absence of feeling — it's the Knight's fast charge meeting uncertainty about where it's going. The fire is real. |
| 2 | King of Chalices (King of Cups) | Approach: emotional groundedness, not suppression. Hold the Knight's excitement inside the King's steady authority. Feel it without letting it run the analysis. |
| 3 | Justice | Approach (continued): strip the evangelical overlay and see what's actually here. Not "is this serious enough to justify continuing" — but what is factually, actually true right now. Precision over projection. |
| 4 | Wheel of Fortune (reversed) | The dynamic: the cycle is mid-spin. The wheel isn't clicking into a clear narrative arc yet — neither "this is the one" nor "this is going nowhere." Genuine in-between. Trying to resolve it into a story from here generates the anxiety. |
| 5 | Ace of Stakes (Ace of Wands) | Despite the reversed Wheel — the spark is real. Something new is genuinely being ignited. Not a dead ember. Both things are true simultaneously: uncertain AND alive. |
| 6 | Three of Stakes reversed (Three of Wands reversed) | Outcome: the ships aren't in yet. The expansion, the "what this becomes" — not yet visible from here. Not canceled, not never. Just not yet formed. |

**Reading summary:** The deck gave an honest answer rather than a reassuring one. The fire is real (Knight of Wands, Ace of Wands). The outcome isn't formed yet (Wheel reversed, Three of Wands reversed). The right move is emotional steadiness and clear seeing, not projecting forward into a story that doesn't exist yet (King of Cups, Justice). The evangelical layer — anxiety asking whether casual enjoyment needs justification — is not in the cards at all. Justice doesn't require that framework. The spark doesn't need justification. It just is.

**Full reading text:**

The first thing notable before interpretation: two pure fire cards (Knight of Wands, Ace of Wands), a major arcana clarity card (Justice), a reversed timing card (Wheel), a reversed expansion card (Three of Wands), and the emotionally mature King of Cups sitting between the fire and the clarity. A very specific shape.

---

**What's going on internally — Knight of Wands**

This is the internal state, and it's honest. The Knight of Wands is the fire of fire — genuine excitement, real attraction, impulse, a "I want this" already in motion in the body. The anxiety last night wasn't coming from absence of feeling. It was coming from the Knight's energy (fast, charged, already moving) running into uncertainty about where it's going. Knights don't sit with ambiguity well. The internal experience is: *I feel this, I'm already moving toward it, and I don't know what it is yet* — and that specific gap is where the anxiety lives. The fire is real. That's what this card is saying first.

---

**How to approach this — King of Cups · Justice**

The King of Cups is everything the Knight of Wands isn't: emotionally grounded, present, not swept away, in authority over his feeling rather than being run by it. He feels deeply — he's not cold — but he holds it steadily. The call is not to suppress the Knight energy but to bring it into the King's container. Feel the excitement, hold it with steadiness. Don't let the anxiety spiral (Knight of Wands at his most reactive) run the analysis.

Then Justice — the heart of this reading. Justice isn't harsh. It's precise. It's *see what's actually here.* In the context of the evangelical wiring that used to show up as guilt and now shows up as anxiety about whether casual enjoyment is "enough" to justify continuing — Justice is a direct counter to that pattern. It's not asking "is this serious enough to keep going." It's asking: what is actually true right now, stripped of the overlay? What's actually true: a genuinely fun first date, real attraction, warmth, a manageable texting dynamic, a second date tonight. Justice asks for assessment from facts, not from the anxiety generating a requirement that wasn't there. The approach: feel the fire (Knight → King), see clearly without the inherited lens (Justice).

---

**What's likely to come from this — Wheel of Fortune reversed · Ace of Wands · Three of Wands reversed**

The Wheel of Fortune reversed is the key card in this cluster. Upright, the Wheel is fate clicking into place, cycles completing, a clear "yes, this is the arc." Reversed: not yet. The cycle is mid-spin. The wheel isn't landing on a clear narrative yet. This isn't the deck saying "it won't work." It's saying the situation is genuinely in-between and trying to resolve it into a clear story from here will generate exactly the anxiety that arrived the night before — because there's no clear story to land on yet.

But then: Ace of Wands. Right after the reversed Wheel. Aces are pure potential, the spark before it becomes a thing. The fire is real, something new is genuinely being ignited, this isn't a dead ember. The deck is not saying "the uncertainty means nothing is here." It's saying "something is here AND it hasn't fully formed yet." Both are true simultaneously.

Three of Wands reversed closes it. Upright, Three of Wands is ships coming in — standing on the cliff watching the horizon, things starting to arrive, expansion becoming visible. Reversed: the ships aren't quite in yet. The vision of "what this becomes" isn't formed. The expansion is delayed or still at sea. Not canceled, not never. Just not yet visible from here.

---

**The arc of the whole reading**

The deck gave an answer to the compatibility anxiety that is honest rather than reassuring. Not: *this is the one, relax.* Not: *this isn't right, move on.* The fire is real (Knight of Wands, Ace of Wands). The outcome isn't formed yet (Wheel reversed, Three of Wands reversed). The right move is emotional steadiness and clear seeing — not projecting forward into a story that doesn't exist yet (King of Cups, Justice).

The Wheel reversed and Three reversed together confirmed the feeling rather than contradicted it. Genuine uncertainty is what's actually there. That's not a problem to solve — it's the correct read of the current state. The evangelical layer — the anxiety asking "does this need to be serious to be justified" — is not in the cards at all. Justice doesn't care about that framework. The spark doesn't need justification. It just is.

**For the date (May 13):** The King of Cups is who to be. Present, warm, not strategizing, not projecting. The Ace of Wands says let the spark be the spark — it doesn't need to become anything bigger tonight than what it actually is. Venus trine natal Saturn exact tonight = structural ease and intellectual pleasure already built into the evening without effort. Nothing needs advancing. Just let it be what it is.
