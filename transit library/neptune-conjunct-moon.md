The colleague who's struggling, you register before they say anything. The project that's actually fine, you feel the fine before the data confirms it. Neptune on the Moon is a permeability upgrade that comes with a cost: the usual distance between intake and overwhelm gets shorter.

Because this is the 10th house Moon, Neptune isn't doing something purely interior. It's thinning the wall between your emotional experience and your professional visibility. A slight, a moment of being uncredited, a project that doesn't land: these land harder than they would otherwise. That's not fragility. It's the transit.

The Aries Moon usually moves fast: impulse, instinct, reaction, then processing. Neptune puts something into the circuit before the impulse fires. A softer transmission speed. Impressions that can be let in before they're converted into decisions. Over the long arc, this is actually useful for an Aries Moon. The tendency toward reactive action gets a slower, more porous counterweight. The vision before the leap.

The most evolved version of this transit is emotional intelligence becoming professional authority. The heightened attunement is data, accurate data from rooms and people and dynamics you're reading correctly. The challenge is learning to use that data with discernment rather than either suppressing it or being submerged by it. Neptune doesn't help with that discernment.
