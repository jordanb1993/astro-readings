# Sagittarius Full Moon Ritual ✦ May 31, 2026
*Blue Moon · Your nodal axis, fully lit*

---

> *The archer doesn't chase the target. She draws back, finds her aim, and releases. The arrow knows where to go.*

## What's Happening in the Sky

This is a **Sagittarius Full Moon** — the Sun in Gemini opposing the Moon in Sagittarius, the polarity that runs directly through your nodal axis. Your North Node sits at 3°02' Sagittarius, your South Node at 3°02' Gemini conjunct your 29° Ascendant. The full moon illuminates exactly the territory your soul came here to inhabit.

It's a **blue moon** — the second full moon in May — which amplifies the charge. Blue moons carry a "what wasn't finished, what wants to complete" quality. This one closes a cycle and opens the summer.

**What the full moon is lighting up in your chart:**
- Your **Sagittarius stellium** (Sun 1°49', North Node 3°02', Mars 10°46') in the 6th house — your daily life, your body, your craft, your work
- Your **South Node/Ascendant in Gemini** — the pattern of performing, mirroring, adapting — being asked to be released
- The **6th house axis** — is your daily life actually aligned with who you are? Not what's expected, but who you are?

This full moon after the week you just had — a launch, social overload, nervous system strain — is asking you to set down the weight of performance and come back to your own signal.

---

## What to Gather

- [ ] One candle — orange, gold, purple, or white (Sagittarius = fire; use what you have)
- [ ] Sage, palo santo, rosemary, or incense — whatever you use to clear
- [ ] Bay leaves (2–3) and something to safely burn them in
- [ ] A pen and paper — not a screen
- [ ] Something for your body: a warm drink, a blanket, comfortable clothes
- [ ] Optional: your tarot deck if you want to pull a card

---

## The Ritual

### 1. Clear the Space *(5 minutes)*

Light your incense or sage. Move through your apartment slowly — bedroom last, since you've just rehung your walls and the space is already in transition. As you move, say aloud or internally:

*"I clear what was. I make room for what is becoming."*

This isn't performative — you're literally walking through a space you've reset this weekend. Let that be part of the ritual.

---

### 2. Ground in Your Body *(3 minutes)*

Sit. Feet flat. Take five slow breaths — longer exhale than inhale.

Feel the week leaving your nervous system. The launch. The social push. The stressful days. Let your body be heavy for a moment. You don't have to carry it into the new season.

---

### 3. Release — Bay Leaf Burning

Write on each bay leaf — one thing per leaf. Then burn them one at a time.

**What to release under this Gemini-Sagittarius axis:**

The question isn't "what's wrong" — it's: *what have I been performing that isn't mine?*

Prompts to choose from:
- The version of yourself that managed how you came across this week — release it
- The social exhaustion of being "on" for other people
- Any story you've been telling about your work, your worth, your pace that came from someone else's framework
- The weight of what hasn't been mailed, filed, sorted — you're handling it this weekend; the anxiety can go

As each leaf burns:
*"I release this. It served me. I'm done with it now."*

---

### 4. Intention — The Archer's Aim

Take a fresh piece of paper. Write at the top: **"By the next Sagittarius full moon, I am..."**

This is your 12-month window. North Node work. What does authentic Jordan — not performing, not adapting, not managing others' comfort — look like in a year?

You don't have to answer perfectly. Write what comes. Some threads to pull:

- The astrology business — first readings, first Etsy listing, first real stranger's chart in your hands
- The summer: Oregon with Carina, Dad's visit, your body getting stronger, dating with some ease
- Your work: what would it look like to be recognized for exactly what you actually do?
- The app, the writing, the creative self — what gets to come out?

When you've written it, fold the paper toward you (drawing in) and keep it somewhere intentional — under your pillow, in your journal, by the candle.

---

### 5. Optional — Tarot Pull

One card. The question: *"What does the archer need to know before she releases?"*

Place it face down first. Breathe. Then turn it over. Let it land before you interpret.

---

### 6. Close

Let the candle burn for a while if it's safe to do so. Sit with the full moon light if you can see it — or just know it's there, doing its work.

Say something that feels true. It can be:
- *"I am on my way."*
- Or just: *"Thank you."*

---

## After the Ritual

Leave the folded intention paper out overnight if you can. In the morning, put it away without re-reading it. The work is done.

The summer starts now.
