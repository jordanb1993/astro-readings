### February 2026 — Compensation Proposal & Rippling Review Meeting
**Source:** Full recorded transcript — `Jordan's Review & 1_1 Sync's transcript.txt`

**What happened:**
In a recorded 1:1 sync, Tony walked Jordan through her Rippling review and her compensation proposal. The conversation documents Tony attempting to suppress Jordan's proposal, misrepresenting Michael's position, and including unsupported negative language in her official HR review — which he removed when challenged.

---

**Part 1: Tony tried to get Jordan to lower and strip her proposal before it reached Michael**

Tony told Jordan her $120K ask was "super aggressive" and that presenting it would make him look bad as a manager. He suggested removing key data points including:
- Market rate / Glassdoor data for Chicago
- The case that she was doing the work of multiple roles
- The email developer cost savings (~$60K/year)

Jordan's response (verbatim from transcript):
> *"Tony, you're suggesting taking out everything that actually proves my worth to the company."*

> *"It feels like pressure and friction in order to get me to back off, and I don't believe that that is necessarily professional."*

> *"I feel like you're trying to tamp down my self-advocacy."*

Jordan held firm and refused to change the proposal. Tony ultimately backed down and agreed to submit it as-is.

---

**Part 2: Tony misrepresented Michael's position**

During the meeting Tony said Michael had called the proposal "out of whack." Jordan immediately caught it:

> **Jordan:** *"Okay, so Michael has said that my proposal is a bit out of whack, it's way too out there, and he has instructed you to work with me to tone it down."*

> **Tony:** *"Uh, he has not said that, but he... I'm pretty much certain he will say that."*

Tony had not spoken to Michael about the proposal at this point. He presented a prediction as fact to pressure Jordan into changing her ask.

---

**Part 3: "Hiccups and missteps" language in the Rippling review — no factual basis**

Tony had included language about "hiccups and missteps" in Jordan's official Rippling review. When Jordan asked for specific examples, Tony offered two:

1. **Sending goals updates to Michael** — Jordan pointed out that Michael was always CC'd on goal communications because Tony himself included Michael on those threads. Tony acknowledged this and could not explain why it was a misstep.

2. **"Overstepping" on masterclasses** — Jordan pointed out that owning the masterclass process end-to-end was explicitly one of her stated objectives. Tony could not dispute this.

When Jordan challenged whether the language should be in her official HR record, Tony removed it in real time:
> *"Yeah, that does make sense. I think that's something I can remove... let me just get rid of that real quick here."*

He also acknowledged:
> *"I just removed it, um, and I think this is awesome where we can talk this out."*

---

**Part 4: Tony admitted being personally upset about the Michael CC**

> *"I think I was kind of a little upset when you did email Michael about this whole thing. I kind of felt that was something that was kind of out of how we normally do things."*

He asked Jordan to come to him first before going to Michael in the future, framing it as a process concern — but the context makes clear it was personal.

---

**How Jordan responded:**
Held her position on the proposal throughout. Pushed back directly and professionally on the Rippling language, secured its removal, and named the pattern plainly without being combative.

**Outcome:**
Promotion confirmed. Effective April 1, 2026: Associate Director of Content Operations. Proposal submitted as-is.

**Why it's relevant:**
- Tony put unsupported negative language into an official HR system and removed it only when directly challenged — establishing that the language had no factual basis
- Tony misrepresented Michael's stated position to pressure Jordan into changing her ask
- Tony explicitly framed Jordan's professional self-advocacy as a personal offense to him
