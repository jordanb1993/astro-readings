## The Premise

The natal chart is not the product. The natal chart is the root. Every section of this app branches from that root — daily sky, long transits, the lunar calendar, the seasonal wheel, relationship readings, the workplace — all interpreted through the specific chart of the person holding it. The architecture exists to make that permanence feel like home.

**One URL. One person's entire astrological life. Navigable, layered, and growing.**

---

## Navigation — Four Tabs (locked June 11, 2026)

```
✦ HOME            ◯ THE CONSTANT      ☽ THE VARIABLE      ✧ THE WHEEL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
portal            natal chart          today's reading      lunar rituals
                  Big Three Portrait   long-term transits   wheel of year
                  area-specific reads  (Daily + Transits    collective sky
                  (static, fixed)       collapsed into one)  (cyclical time)
```

**The naming logic:**

- **The Constant** — Lost S4. Desmond's anchor across all timelines. Your chart is the one fixed thing that never changes. Functionally: natal chart, deep sections, area-specific reads (love/career/body/creativity).

- **The Variable** — Lost S4/5. "We are the variables, we can change destiny." What's moving through your fixed chart right now. Functionally: today's reading + long-term transit archive. Daily and Transits tabs collapse here — you open The Variable, see today, tap a pill, go deeper into that transit.

- **The Wheel** — Triple resonance: the zodiac wheel · the Wheel of the Year (8 Sabbats) · the Frozen Donkey Wheel from Lost (the mechanism that moves the island through time). Functionally: lunar cycles + personalized rituals, wheel of the year personalized to natal chart, collective transit-to-transit sky reading.

**Depth rule:** The Variable is two levels max — today's reading → transit detail. No deeper. The Wheel is a separate tab rather than a nested section of The Variable specifically to prevent depth overload.

## Section Specs

### HOME

The home screen is not a dashboard. It is a threshold. The first thing you see when you open the app.

**What it holds:**
- A current sky moment — one line. Moon sign + phase. The thing happening right now.
- A set of navigation cards, each one a section entry point. Artistic, distinct, not generic tiles.
- A greeting line that changes by time of day, lunar phase, or active transit.

**Card design:**
Each card should feel like a different register — Daily has a different aesthetic weight than Transits which has a different weight than Wheel & Moon. Not the same card reskinned. The cards communicate what kind of experience you're entering.

**Data layer:**
- Moon phase + current sky: `today.json` (already generated by the 4am routine, needs a field added)
- No other dynamic data needed on the home screen

**Build order:** Phase 2. Phase 1 uses the direct chart tabs. Home exists as a concept now, built when there's enough content to navigate between.

---

### DAILY

One reading. Today. Generated fresh by the 4am routine.

**What it holds:**
- Date eyebrow (already exists in current Transits placeholder)
- A "pulse" — one sentence. The weather.
- The day's most significant transit-to-natal contact, named and interpreted.
- 2–3 supporting observations: what's gathering, what's releasing, what to move toward.
- An invitation — one action or awareness to carry into the day.

**How it arrives:**
The 4am CDT routine already generates the daily `.md` and commits to GitHub. The extension: routine also writes `today.json` with the reading content. The HTML fetches it on load. If offline: a graceful fallback shows the most recent cached reading.

**Navigation model:**
- Current reading is default view.
- A small date-picker or left/right arrows lets you scroll back through recent days (last 7 or 30 days stored in the JSON archive).
- Accessing historical readings from within the app is a Phase 2 feature.

**Build order:** Phase 2. The routine already does most of the work.

### TRANSITS

Not a feed. A library you learn to navigate.

**The core UX shift:** Most transit features show you "what's happening." This section shows you "what this is, what it's doing, where it's been, and where it's going." The difference is the difference between a news ticker and a book.

**Top-level view:**
Two lists. Current and Historical. Each transit is a single-line entry:

```
✦  Jupiter trine Pluto        applying · 0.08°        [chevron]
✦  Pluto sextile Moon         separating · 0.29°       [chevron]
◦  Saturn inconjunct Mercury  separating · historical   [chevron]
```

Tap any entry → detail view slides in.

**Detail view (per transit):**
- Transit name + orb + status (applying/exact/separating)
- The full breakdown prose (from the transit library `.md` files — already written)
- Timeline: when it entered orb, when it was exact, when it leaves
- "Passes" — if this is a multi-pass transit (retrograde brings it back), a list of each pass with dates
- Life context: what was happening during each pass — a place to add notes (Phase 3 feature)
- Related transits: other active transits that interact with this one

**Historical archive:**
Past transits are not deleted when they leave orb. They move to the Historical section and stay. The Saturn return. The Neptune chapter. The Jupiter expansion year. These are a permanent record of the transits you've lived through. Over years this becomes an autobiography in transit form.

**Multi-pass transits:**
Saturn, Pluto, Neptune, Uranus — slow planets that retrograde through orb multiple times get a dedicated multi-pass view showing each activation with a timeline.

**Data layer:**
- `transits.json`: index of all active + recent transits, generated by the 4am routine
- Historical transit archive: `transits-history.json` or a Supabase table (Phase 3)
- Breakdown prose: existing transit library `.md` files, either fetched as raw text or pre-serialized into the JSON

**Navigation model:**
Nested nav — list view → detail view → back. No cards. No scroll-feed. The interaction is browsing a living reference library.

**Build order:** Phase 2. The architecture is defined; the routine extension comes first.

### NATAL

The deepest section. Most of this already exists in `jordan.html`. The architecture vision here is what it grows into.

**Current state (built):**
- Chart wheel with pinch-to-zoom
- Identity (Sun, ASC, North Node)
- Depth (Scorpio stellium, Pluto, Mercury, Venus, Jupiter, 7th house)
- Roots (Chiron, IC, 4th house)
- Soul (synthesis, through-line, tarot card, poem)

**The full architecture (Phase 2+):**

#### Chart Wheel *(built)*
The SVG wheel with all placements. Zoomable. Key drawer. Big Three strip.

Future: biwheel mode (natal + transits overlaid). Tap a planet → shows all aspects from that planet.

#### Planet / Sign / House Reading *(built, can go deeper)*
What exists: section cards for the major placements.
What grows into it: a tap on any planet in the wheel or any section header reveals a deeper breakdown — the planet in the sign, the sign in the house, the house's ruler and where it falls. A nested architecture that can go 3 layers deep without being overwhelming.

#### Aspects *(partially built)*
The aspect table already exists. Phase 2: each aspect row expands into an interpretation card. The story of that exact aspect — not just "Sun trine Mars" but "your Sun at 1° Sagittarius trine Mars at 1° Aries, in the fire sign axis, in houses 6 and 10 — this is the aspect that makes public action feel natural."

#### Predictive Patterns *(not yet built)*
The natal chart contains information about recurring themes in a person's life. Not fortune-telling — pattern recognition. The Chiron wound. The South Node pull. The Venus pattern. The Jupiter return cycle. Each of these is a section that interprets the chart in terms of what shows up again and again.

This section translates the birth chart into a roadmap: "Here is what your chart suggests you are working through across a lifetime." This is some of the most psychologically rich content in the whole product.

#### Past Life Reading *(not yet built)*
The South Node, its sign and house, its ruler, the aspects to it — these describe what the soul is releasing. Not metaphorical: treated as genuinely informative about the pattern the person is here to move away from. Jordan's South Node in Gemini (12th) conjunct Uranus is one of the most architecturally interesting placements in the chart.

One section. Prose reading. Probably 300–500 words. Feels like the most mystical part of the natal tab and comes near the end.

#### Tarot Archetype Card *(built in mimi.html — needs to port)*
The single tarot card that maps to the full chart. Already in the canonical template. A visual card + interpretation prose.

#### Verse Poem *(built in mimi.html — needs to port)*
The free verse poem written to the specific chart. Present tense, no em dashes, no technical language, ends on an open question. Jordan's poem exists. Others will be generated fresh per chart.

**Build order:**
- Phase 1: Port tarot card + poem into jordan.html (they're in mimi.html already)
- Phase 2: Predictive Patterns section
- Phase 2: Aspect expansion cards
- Phase 3: Past Life Reading, deep planet-sign-house drill-down

### SYNASTRY & COMPATIBILITY

Relationship charts. Two charts, one reading.

**What it holds:**
- Select two saved charts (from chart vault)
- A composite view: overlapping chart wheels (biwheel)
- Key contacts: the 3–5 most significant synastry aspects with interpretations
- Compatibility notes: where the connection flows, where friction lives
- A composite chart reading (the chart of the relationship itself, not just the two people)

**Modes:**
- Romantic synastry (full depth)
- Friendship synastry (warm, relational)
- Workplace synastry (practical — built for the "how do we work together" question Dasha raised)

**Chart vault:**
Friends' charts are already built — Mimi, Carina, Dasha. The vault in Phase 2 is a list of saved charts you can call up and compare.

**Build order:**
- Phase 1: Jordan's chart vs. friend charts (manually prepared, already doing this)
- Phase 2: In-app synastry with saved charts, basic compatibility view
- Phase 3: User-entered charts, workplace mode, composite chart

### WORKPLACE

Where AstroFlow Weekly lives. Career-forward, practically framed.

**What it holds:**
- The weekly sky-at-work reading (the existing `#astroflow-weekly` format)
- Your natal chart filtered through the career and leadership lens: 10th house, 6th house, Saturn, the MC story
- Timing guidance: when to push, when to wait, when to ask for things
- Optional: workplace synastry (you + a colleague or manager)

**The AstroFlow Weekly format:**
Already built in `CLAUDE-WORKPLACE-HTML.md`. The `sky-at-work.html` is the delivery vehicle. The workplace tab in the personal app surfaces the same content in a native tab context.

**Expansion:**
Over time this section could hold a "professional transit archive" — a personal log of which transits coincided with major career moments. Promotion. Difficult manager. Title negotiation. The app starts to become a career journal keyed to the sky.

**Build order:**
- Phase 1: The workplace tab is a viewer for the current sky-at-work HTML
- Phase 2: Natal career section (10th house, 6th house reading), timing guidance
- Phase 3: Workplace synastry, career journal, team mode

### WHEEL & MOON

Seasonal and lunar cycles. Where the pagan calendar meets the personal chart. Combined because they share a register: ritual, ceremony, invitation.

**Structure:**
Two sub-sections within one tab:

#### The Wheel of the Year
The 8 Sabbats — Samhain, Yule, Imbolc, Ostara, Beltane, Litha, Lughnasadh, Mabon — displayed on a visual wheel.

Each Sabbat has:
- The holiday name + date + what it marks
- General cultural/pagan significance
- **Personalized ritual or celebration** — how this Sabbat interacts with Jordan's specific natal placements. Where is the Sun at this Sabbat, and what house does that activate for her? What natal planet is nearby? What does this moment ask of her specifically?
- An activity or invitation: light a candle at the threshold of your Chiron house. Write a letter to your South Node.

#### The Lunar Section
New moon + full moon, every cycle.

Each moon event has:
- The moon sign + degree + which natal house it falls in
- What this activates in the specific chart
- A personalized ritual: 3–5 steps, ceremonial in tone, specific to this person's chart
- A tarot draw recommendation: which card to pull and sit with for this lunation
- An intention-setting prompt: a question to hold for the next two weeks (new moon) or a reflection prompt (full moon)

**The combination:**
Wheel and Lunar share a visual register — they are both about cyclical time and ceremony. The Wheel is seasonal (8 events/year). The Lunar is monthly (24 events/year). Together they make a complete ceremonial calendar personalized to the natal chart.

**Data layer:**
- Wheel dates: static, calculated once per year
- Lunar events: calculated from ephemeris, included in `today.json` (next new/full moon)
- Ritual content: generated by Claude using natal placement data + the event's astrological context
- Content can be pre-generated seasonally and stored — doesn't need to be real-time

**Build order:**
- Phase 2: Basic lunar section (next new + full moon with personalized ritual)
- Phase 2: Wheel of the Year as a static visual with general content
- Phase 3: Full personalization of both — every event interpreted through the natal chart, ritual library, seasonal journal

## Navigation Architecture

**Bottom nav (current — 7 tabs):**
```
Home · Daily · Transits · Natal · Synastry · Workplace · Wheel
```

This is one more than fits comfortably in a single bottom nav row. Options:
1. Scrollable bottom nav (already built in jordan.html — it scrolls horizontally)
2. Collapse Synastry into Natal (as a sub-section) to keep the nav at 6
3. A "More" overflow button for Workplace + Wheel (lower frequency sections)

**Recommendation:** Keep the scrollable nav. The 7 sections are distinct enough that collapsing them creates confusion. The horizontal scroll is already implemented and works.

**Nested nav within sections:**
Transits, Natal, and Synastry all have nested views (list → detail). The pattern:
- Section landing page = index/overview
- Tap → detail slides in from the right
- Back button (or swipe right) returns to index
- This is a standard iOS modal sheet / push nav pattern — achievable in vanilla JS

## The Governing Principle

*From the 12th House Design Principle in app-vision.md:*

All complexity lives in the invisible layer. What surfaces at the interface is clear, contemplative, and human-feeling. The user should never sense the machinery.

Every architecture decision flows from this. The transit library is complex — the transit tab should feel effortless. The natal chart has 12 houses, 10 planets, hundreds of aspects — the natal tab should feel like reading something written for you by someone who knows you. The wheel has 8 Sabbats and 24 lunations per year — the ritual section should feel like receiving a letter on the right day.

The infrastructure exists to make that possible. Build toward that feeling.
