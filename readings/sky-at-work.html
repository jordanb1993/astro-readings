The Week at a Glance
Short week, compressed intensity

Drive meets resistance all week, ease costs a little more than usual, and the clearest window of leverage lands midweek. The sky's instructions: write it down, push with intention, trust the idea before you have the full argument ready.

Mars □ Pluto Rx Drive pressing against deep structure. Friction is purposeful, not personal.
All Week

Mars is the planet of action and drive; Pluto is the planet of deep, structural power. When they're at a 90° angle (a "square"), forward momentum meets immovable forces. In practice: things that should be moving will push back. A proposal hits an unexpected wall. A process gets stuck. Rather than forcing through it, the better move is to ask what the resistance is pointing at. These are often the weeks when it becomes clear which obstacles are worth fighting and which are protecting something you didn't realize was fragile.

Writing · Documentation · Precision
For Communication
For you this week

Best writing window of the week falls Tuesday and Wednesday. If something was decided in a meeting but never documented, this is the moment to fix that.

The communication planet (Mercury) is at peak strength this week and links up with the structure planet (Saturn) midweek. In plain terms: anything that needs to both move quickly and hold up over time has a real window Tuesday and Wednesday. Proposals, formal follow-ups, contracts, decisions that need to actually stick.

Write it down. Verbal agreements that aren't documented tend to get revisited this week. The paper trail is the point.

Best days: Tuesday and Wednesday. Monday is still in holiday-rhythm diffusion, so loose async is lower quality than usual. Don't wait until Thursday: by then the window has tightened considerably.

Action · Friction · Forward Moves
For Decisions & Momentum
For you this week

Expect pushback this week and don't take it personally. The resistance is telling you something useful about where the real obstacles are.

The action planet (Mars) is in a tense angle with Pluto all week. What this looks like in practice: things that should be moving will push back. A proposal hits an unexpected wall. A process doesn't flow the way it usually does. The instinct is to force through it. The better move is to ask what the resistance is pointing at: whether you're hitting the wrong wall or the right one.

The weeks when drive meets resistance are often when it becomes clear which things are worth the friction and which aren't.

The collaborative window is still open through Wednesday (Venus and Mars are still in a supportive angle from last week). If something needs both goodwill and momentum, earlier in the week has more fuel. By Thursday the "not yet" register gets louder, so don't save big asks for the end of the week.

♀
Connection · Teamwork · Pitching
For Collaboration
For you this week

The room is paying attention this week. Bring substance. Enthusiasm alone won't carry a pitch or a proposal the way it might in a softer week.

The relational planets are in a tense configuration this week, which translates to: people can feel the difference between genuine connection and social performance. Collaborative pitches built on actual substance land. Anything relying primarily on goodwill or on how well you get along with someone will get quietly interrogated.

This isn't a bad week for collaboration. It's a demanding one. The people who've done the work hold the room.

The background pressure all of 2026: the sky keeps asking what the concrete plan is behind the idea. Teams that can answer that question feel this week's pressure as useful. Teams that can't feel it as stall. This week makes that distinction sharper than usual.

Tensions · Timing · Heads Up
Worth Watching

Three things to keep in mind this week.

  • ✦
    Friction is information. If a project keeps hitting walls this week, treat it as data rather than bad luck. Ask what the resistance is telling you about the structure of the problem, rather than just looking for a way around it.
  • ✦
    Tuesday runs hot. Four working days after a holiday compresses. The urge to make Tuesday feel like a catch-up Monday usually produces rushed calls that Thursday has to unwind. Set a deliberate pace early.
  • ✦
    Close open loops in writing. If something got decided verbally in the last two weeks but never formally documented, this week (especially Tuesday and Wednesday) is the ideal window to make it official.
